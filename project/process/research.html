Background Research

When designing a pen pal app targeting intercultural dialogue, it is important to understand the differences between cultures and which similarities can be used to bond them together.

Intercultural dialogue is defined as “an open and respectful exchange of views between individuals, groups with different ethnic, cultural, religious and linguistic backgrounds and heritage on the basis of mutual understanding and respect” (Education to Intercultural Dialogue). To create a respectful and mutual environment where everyone can get along peacefully, there must be effective communication that brings a different perspective into one’s culture. There is a strong need for dialogue, especially now in the United States where this lacking causes fear, tension, and negative stereotypes which further alienate foreign cultures unknown to the native person. We have hostility because of our ignorance and refusal to understand the “ minds of a population whose language and politics” that differ from ours (Herchinger). This is not only apparent in the United States but also in many other counties. “The absence of dialogue deprives everyone of the benefit of new cultural opening, necessary for personal and social development in a globalized world (Education to Intercultural Dialogue). We must then consider how to create a respectful dialogue between people that hold different cultures. Despite being in a cultural melting pot, we can still see much tension and fear stemming from stereotypes perpetrated through the media and lack of personal understanding. The challenge is to be able to take someone is is willing to take a step out of their own social and cultural sphere and leap into a foreign concept willing and open to learn the rules, concepts and ideas of that new space. There are also barriers that prevent this type of cross cultural exploration due to “discrimination, poverty, and […] exploitation” and politics (Education to Intercultural Dialogue). To design something that can overstep such barriers would be ideal in the venture of expanding cultural awareness and understanding.

In addition to understanding what keeps groups from engaging in conversation, we must understand how they understand and interpret interfaces. Due to the nature and form of my project, I must also understand how they interact with interfaces that is different than what I know. How to different cultures perceive certain interfaces? Do things that seem intuitive to one group be the same for the other? Are there certain cultural sensitivities that I should be aware of while I am designing this interface? To answer their questions, I must think about the 6 big differences between two cultures which are: “Power distance, individualism, masculinity, uncertainty avoidance, long-term orientation, and indulgence” (Shen). When you can understand what motivates a certain culture, or if they tend to be more emotional expressive, or non expressive, you can design an interface that best fits their needs and wants. Design is not always universal and understanding the differences in cultural interaction with technology is similar to the need for intercultural dialogue to create a “respectful and mutual environment” (Education to Intercultural Dialogue).

As a designer I am trying to create an interface that promotes healthy conversation among people who differ in many aspects. My goal is to widen each users perspective and provide an interface where they can feel safe to reach out of their comfortable reach in an effort to cast away stereotypes formed from fear of the unknown.
