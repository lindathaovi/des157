ISEA

Comparative Research

For my project, I plan to develop a pen pal application that promotes intercultural dialouge. My mission is to connect strangers all over the world so that they can engage in light or meaningful conversations that will help open up each others perspective.I want people to be able to experience and understand how life is beyond their own scope. To be understand how to implement my idea, I am studying the interfaces and features of other apps offering a pen pal service.

Doonglle

The app Doonglle connects people from all over the world like how pen pals would work. It’s interface lightly resembles a social networking app in that it has a profile for each of it’s user accessible by other’s as well as a “story” area where you can post updates and images for people to see.


At a glace their features include:
  • Story updates
  • Messenging
  • Matches
  • Live Map
  • Snail Mail

They also feature a map that allows you to see people who are online and where they are located. It updates in real-time and bounces you fom location to location when other people sign on. There are buttons that allow you to edit the animations of the map however you prefer.
During the setup phase, Doonglle offered many different preference settings depending on what type of people you wanted to match up with and for what reasons you were using the app. You can restrict the age, gender, langauge, etc. of the people you are matched with.

Interaction

When navigating through the site, it was fairly intuitive. Being a completely new user, it would have been nice to have alert bubbles that would guide a new user through the app as a quick introduction.

The main menu slides out from the left and reveals all the different features the user can access. Something that I noticed was that in the messages inbox or notifications, being a new user, I didn't have any. Rather than displaying a messages to notify the user that there was nothing to display or a suggestion to start a new message, etc. there was only a blank page. Other than those, I thought that the interface was appealing and well-designed. It is quick to understand and the graphics are pleasing.

PSTMRK

POSTMRK is also a pen pal app. This one however, seems much more simple than Doonglle. The site map doesn't seem to be very thorough and the interface seems much less advanced. You are simply working with a pen pal matcher and an inbox and nothing more.


At a glace their features include:
  • Pen pal choice
  • Messenging

As a new user logging in, you create an account using facebook. There is not option to create an account through email which would have been nice. Once, your account has been set up you have limited setting to work with to edit your preferences which are name and gender.

PSTMRK simply allows you to choose a region that you want to select your pen pal from. After deciding on a region, you are directed to a page where "cards" or images appear. You are given a name and their profile picture. Once you decide on who you want to walk to, it leads to you a blank messenger. From there, you can send a message to that person. There is a menu at the bottom that allows you to translate the converstaion, if language is a barrier for you and your pen pal.

Interaction

The site itself is extremely simple and so it wasn't too difficult to figure out what the features were and where they were. Unfortunately I was hoping for more complexity coming from a pen pal app that offered more, beyond simple messenging with strangers.

The interface is slightly dull and the the design overall could use some work. The map is simple and would have been better if it was more animated like doongle or provided live numbers to give the user an idea of their scope.

Overall

After reviewing the two apps, I think that doonglle is more effective in offering a pen pal application and more. It's features adds to the experience which PSTMRK lacks. The ability to put yourself in perspective relative to where you are the the amount of people out there also looking to make connections across the world is awesome.
